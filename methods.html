Methods

Masuda Method

Gen 4 and Up

Breed two Pokemon from games with different language settings to dramatically increase shiny odds. Named after Game Freak director Junichi Masuda, who introduced the mechanic in Diamond and Pearl.

The most common approach is to obtain a foreign Ditto, since Ditto can breed with almost any Pokemon. This makes it easy to apply the Masuda Method to nearly any breedable species without needing a foreign version of that specific Pokemon. A foreign Ditto is not a requirement though — any two compatible Pokemon from different language games will trigger the bonus.

  • Obtain a Pokemon from a game in a different language (trade with a friend or use the GTS)
  • A foreign Ditto is the most flexible option since it can breed with almost anything
  • Breed it with a compatible Pokemon in your game
  • Hatch eggs until a shiny appears
  • Odds: 1 / 682 with Shiny Charm, 1 / 1,365 without

✦ Tip: Use the Oval Charm to produce eggs faster and the Flame Body ability to hatch them in half the steps.

SOS Chaining

Sun and Moon / USUM

In Generation 7, wild Pokemon can call for help during battle. By keeping a chain of the same species alive, shiny odds improve dramatically the longer the chain goes.

To keep the chain going efficiently, use an Adrenaline Orb at the start of the encounter. This item raises the stress level of the wild Pokemon, which makes it significantly more likely to call for an ally each turn. Without one, some Pokemon call rarely and chains can stall out. You only need to use one Orb per battle — using more has no additional effect.

  • Battle a wild Pokemon and use an Adrenaline Orb on the first turn
  • The Adrenaline Orb increases the chance the Pokemon will call for an ally each turn
  • Knock out the ally but keep the original caller alive
  • Repeat until the chain reaches 31 or more calls
  • At 31+ calls odds reach approximately 1 / 315 (with Shiny Charm: 1 / 273)

✦ Tip: Bring Leppa Berries and a Pokemon with Harvest to keep PP topped up during long chains.

DexNav Chaining

ORAS Only

The DexNav in Omega Ruby and Alpha Sapphire lets you search for specific Pokemon. Repeatedly encountering the same species builds a chain that increases shiny odds and Pokemon quality.

  • Use the DexNav search feature to find a target Pokemon
  • Sneak up on it using the Circle Pad slowly
  • Knock it out or catch it to build the chain
  • At 50+ chain: odds reach approximately 1 / 315

✦ Tip: If the DexNav says the Pokemon has a hidden ability, that is a sign of a high search level and better shiny odds.

Outbreak Hunting

Scarlet and Violet

Mass Outbreaks in Scarlet and Violet spawn large numbers of a single species in one area, making them ideal for shiny hunting. The more you defeat in the outbreak, the better the odds get.

  • Find an active Mass Outbreak on the map
  • Defeat or catch 60 of the outbreak Pokemon
  • Use a Sparkling Power sandwich to boost odds further
  • With sandwich and 60 KOs: odds reach approximately 1 / 512

✦ Tip: Make a Sparkling Power Level 3 sandwich before starting to maximize your shiny rate for the full outbreak.

Soft Resetting

All Generations

Soft resetting is the oldest and most universal method. It works on any Pokemon that appears in a fixed location, such as starters, legendaries, and gift Pokemon. You simply reset the game and check again until it appears shiny.

  • Save directly in front of the encounter
  • Trigger the encounter and check if it is shiny
  • If not shiny, soft reset using the combination for your device (see below)
  • Repeat at base odds: 1 / 4,096 (or 1 / 8,192 in older games)
Device / Game Soft Reset Method
Game Boy / GBA (Gen 1, 2, 3) Hold A + B + Start + Select
Nintendo DS / DSi (Gen 4, 5) Hold L + R + Start + Select
Nintendo 3DS (Gen 6, 7, USUM, ORAS) Hold L + R + Start or L + R + Select
Nintendo Switch (Gen 8, 9, Legends) Close the game via the Home menu, then reopen it
Nintendo Switch (GBA Games) Hold A + B + X + Y

✦ Tip: Use the Shiny Counter page to track your resets so you never lose your count.

Chain Fishing

X and Y / ORAS

Chain Fishing is a method exclusive to Generation 6 that involves fishing in the same spot repeatedly without moving or failing a catch. Each consecutive successful reel increases the shiny odds, making it one of the fastest methods available for water-based Pokemon in those games.

The key to keeping the chain going is to never move from your tile, never let a Pokemon escape, and never reel in too early. Breaking any of these rules resets the chain back to zero. Standing next to a wall or corner helps lock your position and reduces accidental movement.

  • Stand in one spot and use any fishing rod repeatedly
  • Successfully reel in and either catch or knock out each Pokemon
  • Do not move, do not miss a reel, and do not let a Pokemon flee
  • At a chain of 20 or more the shiny rate reaches approximately 1 / 200
  • Works in both X and Y and Omega Ruby and Alpha Sapphire

✦ Tip: Use a Pokemon with the Suction Cups or Sticky Hold ability in the lead slot to reduce the chance of getting a no-bite reel, which would break your chain.

Random Encounters

All Generations

Sometimes you just walk through tall grass, surf, or explore a cave and hope for the best. Full odds random encounter hunting is the purest form of shiny hunting and can apply to any wild Pokemon in any game.

  • No setup required beyond saving in the right area
  • Hold the Shiny Charm in Gen 6 and later to improve odds
  • Use Repels to target specific encounter slots if available
  • Odds: 1 / 4,096 normally, 1 / 1,365 with Shiny Charm

✦ Tip: Pretty simple, just hope you get lucky.
